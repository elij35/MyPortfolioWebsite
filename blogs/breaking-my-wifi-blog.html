How I Broke My Own Wi-Fi

A cautionary tale of misconfigured DHCP servers and network chaos

It started as a simple experiment to optimize my home network. Little did I know I was about to take down internet access for every device in my apartment...

The Setup

I had recently acquired a Raspberry Pi and decided to repurpose it as a network controller. My goal was to implement more granular control over my IoT devices while improving security. The plan seemed straightforward:

  • Set up a local DNS server
  • Configure DHCP to assign static IPs to trusted devices
  • Implement VLAN segmentation

The Mistake

In my enthusiasm, I made two critical errors:

  1. I didn't properly document my original router settings
  2. I enabled the Pi's DHCP server without disabling the router's DHCP service

The result? A classic case of DHCP server conflict that immediately caused all connected devices to lose internet access as they received conflicting IP assignments.

The Recovery

After 30 minutes of panic, I systematically:

  1. Disconnected the Pi from the network
  2. Factory reset my router
  3. Reconfigured all devices manually

The entire ordeal taught me a valuable lesson when changing network settings:

Always maintain a backup configuration and make changes incrementally. What seems like a simple tweak can have unforeseen effects on network stability.
